What Is Dance?

Dance is an art form, consisting of sequences of body movements with aesthetic and often symbolic value,
either improvised or purposefully selected.

Dance can be categorized and described by its choreography,
by its repertoire of movements or by its historical period or place of origin.

Dance is typically performed with musical accompaniment,
and sometimes with the dancer simultaneously using a musical instrument themselves.

Interesting Benefits:

Dancers are known to be disciplined, focused and high achievers who tend to be successful students and hard workers.

Research also proves that dancing also reduces stress and tension for the mind and body.
Studies by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute have shown that dancing also prevents heart disease in particular.

Dancers also have increased muscular strength, endurance and motor fitness. (No wonder professional dancers are today regarded as athletes.)

Because of high physical demand on their bodies, most professional dances retire from dancing during their mid-30s.

They also have better coordination, agility and flexibility.

5 Tips For Beginner Dancers:

Start with the Basics:

Focus on learning basic movements and positions first. Mastering the foundation is crucial before progressing to more complex steps. Whether it's ballet, hip-hop, or any other style, understanding core principles will give you a solid base.

Stay Consistent:

Dance is a skill that requires practice. Set aside regular time to practice, even if it's just for 15-20 minutes each day. Consistency helps build muscle memory and improves your technique over time.

Warm Up and Stretch:

Always warm up before dancing. Stretching prevents injuries and improves flexibility, which is key for many styles.

Focus on Rhythm:

Dance is all about music and timing. Practice with music to improve your sense of rhythm and how your movements align with the beat.

Be Patient and Enjoy:

Progress takes time, so don't rush it. Enjoy the learning process and don't get discouraged by mistakes—they're part of the journey!
